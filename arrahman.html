A.R.RAHMAN


About

A.R. Rahman, often referred to as the "Mozart of Madras," is an internationally acclaimed Indian composer, singer, and music producer. Born on January 6, 1967, in Chennai (formerly Madras), India, Rahman is known for his work in the Indian film industry, as well as in international cinema and music. Early Life: Rahman, born as Allah Rakha Rahman, showed an interest in music from an early age. His father was a composer, and he was influenced by various musical traditions, including Indian classical, Western classical, and world music. After his father's death, Rahman turned to music more seriously. He initially studied at the Madras Conservatory of Music and later trained under renowned musicians in both Western and Indian classical music. Career: Rahmanâ€™s breakthrough in the film industry came with the soundtrack of the 1992 Tamil film Roja, directed by Mani Ratnam. The album was a huge success, earning Rahman recognition as a talented new composer in Indian cinema. He quickly became known for his unique blend of classical music with electronic music, world rhythms, and innovative arrangements. Following Roja, Rahman went on to create music for numerous other films, both in Tamil and Hindi, such as Dil Se, Taal, Lagaan, and Guru. His soundtracks for Bollywood and Tamil cinema earned him widespread acclaim, and his music became an essential part of the Indian film experience.

ACHIEVEMENTS

A.R. Rahman's Achievements

  • Oscar Wins:
    • Best Original Score for Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
    • Best Original Song for the song Jai Ho from Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
  • Grammy Awards:
    • Best Compilation Soundtrack for Visual Media for Slumdog Millionaire (2010)
    • Best Song Written for Visual Media for Jai Ho (2010)
  • National Film Awards: 6 National Film Awards, including Best Music Direction for various films.
  • Padma Bhushan (2010): Awarded India's third-highest civilian honor for his contribution to music.
  • Filmfare Awards: 15 Filmfare Awards, including Best Music Director in various Indian languages.
  • BAFTA Award: Best Original Music for Slumdog Millionaire (2009).
  • Golden Globe: Best Original Score for Slumdog Millionaire (2009).
  • International Recognition: Known globally for his work on projects like The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers and his collaborations with international artists.
  • Mumbai's Maratha Mandir's "Jai Ho" Anthem: Jai Ho used as an anthem for various occasions, including the 2010 Commonwealth Games closing ceremony.
  • Documentary: *The Mozart of Madras*, a documentary about his life and career.
  • Musical Albums: Iconic albums like Taal, Dil Se, Lagaan, Guru, Rockstar, and Dil Se.
  • Other Honors: Named in *Time 100* Most Influential People (2009) and awarded the Czech Republic's National Medal of Arts.
